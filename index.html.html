The Annotation Problem Nobody Talks About

Most medical AI models don't fail because of bad architecture. They fail because of bad data.

When your training images are labeled by annotators who've never held a transducer or read a DICOM file, the model learns the wrong things — confidently. Missed lesion boundaries. Misclassified pathology. Anatomical structures labeled by guesswork.

That doesn't show up in your loss curve. It shows up when the model meets a real patient.

Bad annotation isn't a minor QA issue. It's a six-figure retraining cost, a delayed FDA clearance, or worse — a clinical tool that nobody trusts enough to deploy.

The dataset you're training on right now — do you actually know who labeled it?
